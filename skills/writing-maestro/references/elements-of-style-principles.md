# Elements of Style Principles

Use this as a copyediting checklist for clear and concise prose.
This is a compact, practical adaptation of Strunk's style rules and common usage guidance.

## Core Rules

1. Form the possessive singular with apostrophe + s.
2. In a series, separate elements clearly.
3. Parenthetical expressions must be enclosed by matching punctuation.
4. Place a comma before a coordinating conjunction that joins independent clauses.
5. Do not join independent clauses with only a comma.
6. Keep pronoun case correct.
7. Use participial phrases only when they clearly modify the intended noun.
8. Keep one tense in summaries unless a real time shift is needed.
9. Put statements in positive form.
10. Use active voice by default.
11. Prefer specific, concrete language.
12. Omit needless words.
13. Avoid loose strings of qualifying words and stock intensifiers.
14. Use parallel construction for equivalent ideas.
15. Keep related words together.
16. In summaries, keep one grammatical subject when possible.
17. Place emphatic words near the end of the sentence.
18. Keep to a single level of formality and tone unless the format requires a shift.

## High-Impact Rewrite Checklist

- Can this sentence be shorter without losing meaning?
- Is the actor clear, or is passive voice hiding responsibility?
- Are claims concrete, verifiable, and specific?
- Are there empty transitions like "it is worth noting" or "importantly"?
- Are two adjacent sentences doing the same job?
- Are repeated openings creating mechanical rhythm?
- Would a direct verb outperform a noun-heavy phrase?

## Usage Pitfalls to Watch

- `can` (ability) vs `may` (permission)
- `fewer` (countable) vs `less` (mass)
- `that` (defining clause) vs `which` (non-defining clause)
- `who` (subject) vs `whom` (object)
- `each other` (two) vs `one another` (more than two)
- `different from` is safer than `different than`

## Compression Patterns

- Replace noun-heavy constructions with verbs.
  - "made an adjustment to" -> "adjusted"
- Delete throat-clearing lead-ins.
  - "It is important to note that" -> remove or state the point directly.
- Collapse duplicated qualifiers.
  - "very unique and highly distinctive" -> "distinctive"

## Application Order

1. Fix correctness issues first.
2. Remove redundancy and filler.
3. Improve clarity with concrete language and active verbs.
4. Tune cadence and emphasis in final polish.
